# Engineering Knowledge Base

## Purpose

The Engineering Knowledge Base serves as the permanent record of the engineering systems, architecture, lessons learned, and technical decisions developed throughout my career.

Unlike a resume, this document is intentionally comprehensive.

It is designed to preserve engineering knowledge so that future resumes, technical portfolios, articles, presentations, and engineering decisions can all be generated from a single trusted source.

Nothing important should ever be forgotten because it was never documented.

# Engineering Domains

## Identity Engineering

Identity is the foundation of modern security.

Throughout my engineering career I have consistently viewed identity as more than authentication.

Identity provides the context required to understand access, privilege, ownership, accountability, and ultimately organizational risk.

Areas of engineering include:

• Microsoft Entra ID
• Identity Governance
• RBAC Architecture
• Privileged Access
• Service Account Lifecycle
• Microsoft Graph
• Identity Synchronization
• Identity Correlation
• Identity Visibility
• Identity Automation

Engineering Philosophy

Better identity engineering improves every downstream security decision.

## Detection Engineering

Detection engineering should improve understanding—not simply increase alerts.

The objective is to create actionable visibility that allows engineers to investigate meaningful events while minimizing unnecessary operational noise.

Areas of engineering include:

• Microsoft Sentinel
• KQL
• Threat Hunting
• Detection Logic
• Investigation Workflows
• Dashboards
• Security Analytics
• MITRE ATT&CK Mapping
• Operational Monitoring

Engineering Philosophy

Detection quality is determined by context, engineering discipline, and operational understanding—not alert volume.
