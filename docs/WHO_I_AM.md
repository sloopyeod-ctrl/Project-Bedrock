# Who I Am

My name is Marvin Dewitt.

I'm a Security Systems Engineer.

More importantly, I'm someone who enjoys making complicated systems simpler.

Throughout my career I've found myself asking the same question over and over again.

"Why are talented people spending so much time doing work that engineering should already have solved?"

That question followed me through military service, enterprise security engineering, identity governance, cloud security, Linux engineering, detection engineering, and eventually into building the Unified Security Operations Platform (USOP).

I don't enjoy complexity for the sake of complexity.

I enjoy understanding systems well enough to simplify them.

Whether that means automating repetitive work, engineering identity governance, designing security baselines, improving detection capabilities, or building entirely new platforms, the objective is always the same.

Give engineers more time to solve meaningful problems.

Throughout more than twenty-two years in the Army and later as a Security Engineer, I've learned that technology is rarely the hardest part.

People are.

Processes are.

Communication is.

Engineering exists to improve all three.

Project Bedrock exists because I wanted to preserve that philosophy.

USOP exists because I wanted to apply it.

I don't expect every engineer to solve problems the same way I do.

But I do believe every engineer has a responsibility to leave the next engineer with stronger systems than they inherited.

That's the kind of engineer I continue striving to become.
