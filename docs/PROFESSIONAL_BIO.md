# Professional Biography

## Marvin G. Dewitt

I am a Security Systems Engineer focused on designing systems that reduce operational complexity and allow security professionals to spend more time protecting infrastructure.

Throughout my career, I have been less interested in maintaining technology than improving it.

Whether working in military operations, enterprise cloud security, identity governance, Linux engineering, detection engineering, or automation, I have consistently looked for opportunities to replace repetitive manual work with scalable engineering solutions.

My engineering philosophy is simple:

> Leave every system better than you found it.

That principle has guided every stage of my career.

During more than twenty-two years as a United States Army Explosive Ordnance Disposal technician and leader, success depended on disciplined planning, technical problem solving, continuous learning, and making sound decisions under pressure.

Those same principles continue to guide my engineering work today.

As a Security Engineer, I have designed enterprise identity governance processes, Microsoft Sentinel detection capabilities, Linux security baselines, cloud security automation, vulnerability management workflows, and engineering standards supporting highly regulated environments.

Today I am also the founder and principal engineer behind the Unified Security Operations Platform (USOP).

USOP began with a simple observation.

Security engineers spend too much of their time collecting information instead of solving security problems.

Identity exists in one system.

Infrastructure exists in another.

Threat intelligence exists somewhere else.

Vulnerability data lives somewhere else.

The engineer becomes responsible for manually connecting everything before meaningful work can even begin.

I believe that model is broken.

USOP is my effort to change it.

Rather than creating another dashboard or another source of alerts, the platform is being engineered to become a decision intelligence system that helps engineers understand:

• What matters.
• Why it matters.
• Which systems are affected.
• What protections already exist.
• What action should happen next.

Everything I build follows the same philosophy.

Reduce unnecessary complexity.

Increase engineering effectiveness.

Preserve knowledge.

Automate repetitive work.

Build systems that continue creating value long after implementation.

Project Bedrock exists to document that journey.

It is my engineering notebook, my professional operating system, and the foundation from which every future article, portfolio, resume, presentation, and engineering decision will be built.

I don't believe engineering is measured by the number of technologies someone knows.

I believe it is measured by the quality of the systems they leave behind.

That is the engineer I continue working to become.
